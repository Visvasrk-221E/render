Introduction

Definition of Biotechnology


Biotechnology deals with the techniques of using live organisms or enzymes from organisms to produce products and processes that are useful to humans. It gave a definition which encompasses both the traditional view as well as the modern molecular biology.

"The integration of natural science and organisms, cells, parts thereof, and molecular analogues for products and services is known as biotechnology."
Examples of Biotechnology :

  • In sense all microbe mediated processes, making of curd, bread, wine could also be thought of biotech, but presently it is used in restricted sense.
  • Only those processes which use GMO(Genetically Modified Organisms) to achieve large scale production of biotech products are so considered into definition.
  • In vitro fertilisation, ie test tube baby
  • Synthesising a gene and using it
  • Developing a vaccine against a viral disease and using it
  • Correcting a defective gene...
  • All the above mentioned are part of biotech

Principles of Biotechnology

Two Core Biotechnolgy Techniques


[1] Genetic Engineering :
Techniques to alter the genetic chemistry and to introduce these into the host organisms and change their phenotype.

[2] Bioprocess Engineering :
Maintainence of sterile environment in chemical engineering process to enable the growth of only desired microbe (or) eukaryotes in large quantities for the manufacturing of biotechnological products like antibiotics, vaccines, enzymes, etc.

Some Basic Concepts


[1] Recombinant DNA or rDNA
DNA molecule that is ligated into a vector and then inserted into a host organism to produce new genetic conditions or combinations and thus expresses the new phenotype is called as Recombinant DNA.

[2] Construction of the first artificial recombinant DNA molecule
Organisms Used : Salmonella Typhimurium, E. Coli.
Done by : Stanley cohen and Herbert Boyer
Year : 1972
Theory :

  • First artificial recombinant DNA was produced by Stanley Cohen and Herbert Bayer.
  • They first isolated an antibiotic resistance gene from a bacteria using the specific 'Molecular Scissiors' known as restriction enzymes and joined them to the plasmid vector of Salmonella typhimurium using the DNA ligase enzyme.
  • This newly formed modified DNA plasmid which carries the fragments of antibiotic resistance gene is called as the Recombinant DNA.
  • This plasmid of Salmonella typhimurium with the gene of an antibiotic resistance factor acts as a DNA vector and is imported into the bacterium Escherichia coli.
  • The plasmid was replicated in the E.coli with help of DNA polymerase and made multiple copies of itself, known as cloning.
