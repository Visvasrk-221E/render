Subject Description

SceneScape begins by validating and profiling CSV datasets using the AI’s native data analysis features to parse tabular inputs, summarize distributions, uncover correlations, and prepare derived features that capture trends, segments, and anomalies suitable for downstream scenario logic. The analysis environment is designed for .csv and .txt files and commonly uses Python/pandas under the hood, enabling group‑bys, time‑series transforms, and lightweight modeling that the AI can surface transparently for review and iteration. Extracted signals are then mapped into a STEEP framing (Social, Technological, Economic, Environmental, Political) to structure drivers and uncertainties before scenario drafting, ensuring that narratives are anchored to external forces rather than raw datapoints alone.

Using a generative AI scenario‑planning approach, SceneScape converts these drivers into multiple plausible pathways with explicit hypotheses, assumptions, branch points, and early‑warning indicators to link foresight with timely action. To maintain plausibility, drafts are stress‑tested with adversarial prompts and likelihood–impact lenses, drawing on best practices that show gen AI can accelerate scenario creation while benefiting from structured constraints and review. Final outputs are concise narrative vignettes with indicator sets, decision triggers, and implications that support agile planning and rapid updates as new CSVs or signals arrive, keeping scenarios dynamic rather than static forecasts.
